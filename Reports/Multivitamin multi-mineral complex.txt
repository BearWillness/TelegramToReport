Extensive research has explored the potential benefits of multivitamin multi-mineral complexes on human health and longevity. Studies have indicated that certain nutrients included in these supplements, such as vitamin D and calcium, may aid in improving bone health and reducing the risk of osteoporosis. While multivitamin multi-mineral complexes hold promise for augmenting health and longevity, it is critical to consider potential risks and limitations associated with their use. Excessive consumption of certain nutrients, particularly fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, and K) and minerals like iron, can lead to toxicity and adverse health effects. Furthermore, some studies have raised concerns about the bioavailability and absorption of nutrients in supplement form compared to those obtained through whole foods. Consequently, it is essential to consult healthcare professionals before initiating multivitamin multi-mineral supplementation to ensure appropriate dosing and avoid interactions with medications or underlying health conditions.
